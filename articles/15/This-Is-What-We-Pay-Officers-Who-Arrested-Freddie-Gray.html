This is How Much We Paid Officers Involved in the Freddie Gray Debacle

April 26, 2015

The city released the names of the six officers involved in Freddie Gray’s arrest and subsequent death. Below is how much each of them earned in fiscal year 2014 based on Open Baltimore data.

The six officers have been suspended with pay. Assuming a 40 hour work week and 48 working weeks in the year, below are these officers’ hourly wages, daily wages, and assuming a 50% ratio of salary for benefits and insurance (typical assumption for wage calculations), the six officers are costing Baltimore City taxpayers $2,151.44 every day of their suspension. Today is April 26 and as far as I can tell from the earliest Associated Press announcement of their suspension, the officers have been suspended for six days, so the current bill to taxpayers is $12,908.63 and counting.


Data obtained from Open Baltimore.
Analysis conducted using Python, Pandas, and Plot.ly.
